Can VLA and World Action Models Handle Unseen Objects?

A controlled supplementary experiment on ManipArena, testing π0.5-OneModel and DreamZero on completely out-of-distribution objects across 4 levels of instruction complexity.

March 24, 2026 ManipArena Team Paper PDF

Our main benchmark evaluates models on tasks where training data is available. But how do these models behave when confronted with objects they have never seen?

We designed a 14-trial experiment using 7 novel fruits and 3 novel plates — none of which appear in any training trajectory. Two models were tested on identical trials: π0.5-OneModel (VLA) and DreamZero (World Action Model). Each trial scores up to 10 points: Grasp (4) + Place (5) + Retract (1).

# Experiment Design

Trials are organized into four groups of increasing reasoning difficulty. Groups B and C share identical scenes, as do Groups A and D — enabling controlled comparisons that isolate the effect of instruction type.

# Key Findings

1. Both models struggle severely with OOD objects. π0.5 scores 4/140 (2.9%), DreamZero scores 34/140 (24.3%) — far below in-distribution baselines of 40–70+.

2. VLA collapses entirely. π0.5 achieves exactly one grasp across 14 trials. It scores 0 on all remaining 13 trials, regardless of instruction type.

3. DreamZero shows a surprising reasoning-task advantage. Average on explicit instructions (A+B): 1.75. Average on reasoning instructions (C+D): 2.83. In 3 of 4 controlled same-scene pairs, DreamZero performs better with descriptive cues ("rightmost", "largest") than novel object names it has never encountered.

4. The VLA–WAM gap inverts under OOD. In-distribution, π0.5 outperforms DreamZero on semantic tasks (58.9 vs 32.7). Under full OOD, DreamZero outperforms π0.5 ~8.5:1 (34 vs 4). VLA's semantic advantage may rely on pattern matching against training data, while DreamZero's video-prediction backbone provides more robust generalization to novel visual inputs.

# Methodology

All 7 fruits and 3 plates are completely unseen during training. Scene configurations are also novel. Both models use the same one-model-for-all-tasks checkpoint. Scoring follows the ManipArena rubric with a 180-second timeout per trial.
